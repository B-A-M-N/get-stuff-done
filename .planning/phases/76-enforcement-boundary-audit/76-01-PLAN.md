## Locked Decisions

Decision: audit scope includes CLI routes, internal libraries, and direct authoritative file writes.
Decision: findings classify as `validator_bypass`, `writer_bypass`, `execution_bypass`, or `truth_claim_bypass`.
Decision: authoritative surfaces are explicitly enumerated and must route through sanctioned interfaces.
Decision: critical bypasses require runtime proof or disproof.
Decision: sanctioned interfaces and required validators are declared in policy files, not inferred.
Decision: Phase 76 outputs both `.planning/audit/enforcement-boundary.json` and `76-VERIFICATION.md`.

## Risks

Risk: the audit can over-report by flagging internal implementation details that are actually sanctioned through a higher-level helper.
Guardrail: keep sanctioned interfaces explicit in policy and treat raw fs writes as acceptable only when they occur inside declared writer modules.
Fallback: if a candidate bypass cannot be proven critical, classify it conservatively as MAJOR or static-only rather than claiming exploitability.

## Assumptions

Assumption: current sanctioned mutation surfaces in `state.cjs`, `roadmap.cjs`, and verification helpers are the likely baseline policy entries, but they must still be declared explicitly.
Assumption: targeted runtime probes can be implemented against a temp project without mutating the real repo outside audit artifacts.
Deferred: broader remediation and operator ergonomics belong to later phases once the audit surface is proven.
