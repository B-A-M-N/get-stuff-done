## Locked Decisions

Decision: operator-facing degraded state is restricted to `HEALTHY | DEGRADED | UNSAFE`.
Decision: `UNHEALTHY` and other raw labels must be normalized into the canonical policy vocabulary before operator presentation.
Decision: Phase 75 must write `.planning/health/latest-degraded-state.json` as the canonical degraded-policy artifact.
Decision: `brain health --raw` and `health degraded-mode` must consume one shared degraded-policy truth rather than recomputing state independently.
Decision: missing or stale drift or reconciliation truth is enforceable degraded state rather than informational only.
Decision: diagnostic and status commands remain runnable under `UNSAFE`, while truth-bearing flows may block.

## Risks

Risk: multiple health surfaces can diverge if each computes degraded posture independently.
Guardrail: centralize normalization and artifact writing in one policy module and make reader surfaces consume it.
Fallback: if a raw condition is ambiguous, normalize conservatively to the lowest defensible trust state rather than presenting healthy optimism.

## Assumptions

Assumption: `.planning/health/latest-degraded-state.json` is the canonical runtime artifact for degraded policy truth and is separate from drift observation and reconciliation artifacts.
Assumption: live subsystem health plus drift/reconciliation freshness is sufficient to compute the Phase 75 policy snapshot without adding a new infrastructure service.
Deferred: exact freshness thresholds may be codified during implementation so long as they are deterministic, tested, and documented in the module contract.
