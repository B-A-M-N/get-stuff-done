## Locked Decisions

Decision: Mocked logic presented as real, bypass paths, unimplemented critical branches, and execution-affecting placeholder logic are automatic blockers and force `INVALID`.
Decision: `TODO`, `FIXME`, stubs, and non-critical dead code are degraders and force at least `CONDITIONAL`.
Decision: Verification must actively tag inconsistencies as `spec_drift`, `implementation_drift`, `verification_drift`, or `execution_drift`; placeholder drift sections are not allowed.
Decision: Out-of-scope or grandfathered legacy findings are recorded as non-blocking historical drift rather than current-phase blockers.

## Risks

Risk: anti-pattern scanning can overreach and incorrectly block verification on historical or out-of-scope findings.
Guardrail: classify current-scope blockers separately from non-blocking historical drift, and require explicit tagging instead of heuristic prose.
Fallback: if the validator cannot prove a finding is in current scope, keep it recorded as non-blocking historical drift rather than silently ignoring it or over-upgrading it into an active blocker.
