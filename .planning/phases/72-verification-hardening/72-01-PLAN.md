## Locked Decisions

Decision: Verification final status is restricted to `VALID`, `CONDITIONAL`, or `INVALID`, and any invalid requirement forces final status `INVALID`.
Decision: Every requirement row must cite direct evidence such as commit, file, test output, or runtime output; summaries may only point at lower-level proof.
Decision: Human escalation is structured and machine-readable, but it never counts as evidence and blocks `VALID` while unresolved.
Decision: `VERIFICATION.md` is purely evidentiary and must not embed fix-plan generation.

Risk: the current verification scaffold and parser still reflect the older `passed | gaps_found | human_needed` model and may permit narrative-only success.
Guardrail: harden the existing template and verification helpers in place rather than introducing a parallel verification format.
