## Locked Decisions

Decision: reuse the Phase 72 evidence-first verification contract; do not invent a second verification schema.
Decision: summaries and `TRUTH.yaml` are claim maps and scoping aids only; they are never sufficient proof on their own.
Decision: no Phase 80 task may fabricate historical evidence or imply that a historical action was reproven when only current durable behavior is observable.
Decision: any claim that cannot be re-established directly must remain an explicit gap and keep the relevant row or final verdict at `CONDITIONAL` or `INVALID`.
Decision: Phase 80 closes missing verification artifacts only; it does not rewrite summaries, truth artifacts, roadmap bookkeeping, or requirement traceability.
Decision: a reusable retro-verification helper is allowed only because it materially reduces format drift across five artifacts; it must gather evidence and derive status, not auto-promote claims.

## Risks

Risk: the helper could become a second truth engine that hides weak evidence behind generated prose.
Guardrail: keep helper output limited to checklist assembly, status derivation, and markdown rendering from explicit evidence records.
Fallback: if helper logic starts to obscure evidence attribution, reduce it to thin formatting utilities and write the phase files directly.

Risk: summary proof indexes could be copied verbatim into verification artifacts and mistaken for present-tense proof.
Guardrail: every cited summary item must be revalidated through a current file, command, test, or machine artifact check before it appears in `Evidence`.
Fallback: unresolved claims stay in `Drift Analysis` or `Notes` as unproven, never as `VALID`.

Risk: current degraded posture and unavailable planning infrastructure may make some historical behavior only partially provable.
Guardrail: record infrastructure limitations explicitly in `Anti-Pattern Scan` or `Drift Analysis` and downgrade verdicts mechanically.
Fallback: prefer truthful `CONDITIONAL` closeout over optimistic `VALID`.

## Assumptions

Assumption: the repo still contains enough durable implementation, CLI, and test surfaces to verify the enduring outcomes of phases 72, 73, and 74.
Assumption: the Phase 72 validator surface can validate retroactively created verification artifacts without additional schema work.
